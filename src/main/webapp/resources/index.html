The perfect applicant for the job

The technique of finding the right person for the job has evolved over the last decades, from paper printed CVs that were delivered to the company, to websites that make available the publication of job openings, even to the creation of a social online platform where employees can update their CV in the hopes of a company to stumble upon them(LinkedIn).

But the premises remained the same the employee keeps up to date a certain type of CV out of many formats; the company is fortunate to stumble upon the CV and if the requirements for the job are met, a meeting may be scheduled.

This is a platform where an employer can literally fill the blanks with requirements of a desired future employee, and after that a whole list of people who meet those requirements will be shown to him. In this way there is a much simpler screening process of the market which conveys into a much higher chance of hiring the right person for the job. The implications are huge, because it would emphasize experience, skills and knowledge over the opportunity of some persons CV being more available the others. This in turn will produce a well-made hierarchy of skill and also push newcomers to be better, learn and achieve more because then they will have a visible higher change of getting the job they want.
